Why Green Bangle Movement matters

Bangladesh’s coastline sits at the front line of sea-level rise, cyclones and salinity. The communities who live there are often women shouldering unpaid care work, fragile incomes and the first impacts of climate shocks. GBM is our response: gentle, practical, rooted in place and people.

Climate

Living coastal infrastructure

Mangroves and palmyra palms slow down waves, hold soil together and protect freshwater. They are low-cost, repairable and adapt with time — unlike concrete walls.

Justice

Centering those most affected

GBM treats coastal women not as beneficiaries but as co-designers, trainers and custodians of local resilience strategies.

Culture

Reviving palmyra culture

Every palmyra grove carries crafts, stories and skills. Restoring the trees also restores memory and reduces dependence on cheap, fragile plastics.

Green Bangle Movement

Rooted in care, our work stretches from seedlings to shorelines and from women’s circles to policy tables.
